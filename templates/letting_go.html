Letting Go

{% endblock %} {% block poem %}

I sit in the cabin
My arms spread apart
I listen to music
The beats of my heart.

I listen to the rhythm
It is steady and slow
The people are calming down
My destination unknown.

Music changes my soul
I escape into another planet
I change my playlist from pop to classic
For I realize I am altering a habit.

And so I sit in my seat
Awaiting my final destination
When out of the blue
My heart yells frustration.

My fingers grasp the railing
My hair is flying in the air
My feet above the ground
While my eyes are shedding tears.

I pretend that I'm shooting a film
And I'm just finishing my act
But then reality hits me
And I'm blown away by its powerful impact.

I hold on tight
So I do not lose my grip
But then I start feeling awful
I start regretting the trip.

The cars are connected to each other
So there is no option of running away
So I fight with all my might
To get to see the light of another day.

I watch the moon, the stars fulfill their duty
I watch the waves splashing at sea
But only five of my fingers are now on the railing
For no one seems to answer my plea.

And then I feel like evil is on its way
It wishes to grab my soul
It wishes to thrust its fragments on the ground
And shatter its life's purpose and goal.

And the angels dressed in black
With their capes spreading darkness through the air
Pounce on my back nonstop
For they want me to drown in despair.

For a moment I think it's too late
For all ten fingers have left the railing But then G-d got hold of my soul
And so I felt secure, even though I've been failing.
